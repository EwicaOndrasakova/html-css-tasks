First Steps into Space Tourism

Not long ago, a trip to space was reserved only for trained astronauts. Today, private companies like SpaceX, Blue Origin, and Virgin Galactic are opening the door to civilians. Early "space excursions" are short journeys above the Kármán line, offering just a few minutes of weightlessness and a breathtaking view of Earth from more than 100 km up.

A Day Aboard a Spacecraft

Imagine sleeping in a floating sleeping bag strapped to the wall instead of a hotel bed. Breakfast comes in sealed pouches, and water is sipped from bags—cups simply don't work in microgravity. During the day, passengers gaze through windows at the Earth below, photograph the endless black of space, and enjoy the surreal feeling of drifting without weight.

From the Moon to Mars

Today's space excursions may last only minutes or days, but the future looks much further. NASA and private companies are already planning missions to the Moon, building bases for longer stays, and setting their sights on Mars. These journeys will push human limits and transform space tourism from a short thrill into true interplanetary exploration.
